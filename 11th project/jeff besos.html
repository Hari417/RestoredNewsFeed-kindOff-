Early Life

Bezos showed an early interest in how things work, turning his parents' garage into a laboratory and rigging electrical contraptions around his house as a child.

Bezos graduated from Princeton University in 1986 with a degree in computer science and electrical engineering.

It was during his high school he took his first step into buisness arena,an Educational camp for fourth, fifth and sixth graders.Then he undertook a series of jobs bofore joining the New York invesment bank D.E.Shaw&Co in 1990.Soon named senior vice president—the firm’s youngest—Bezos was in charge of examining the investment possibilities of the Internet. Its enormous potential—Web usage was growing by more than 2,000 percent a year.

In 1994 he quit D.E. Shaw and moved to Seattle, Washington, to open a virtual bookstore. Working out of his garage with a handful of employees, Bezos began developing the software for the site. Named after the South American river, Amazon sold its first book in July 1995.
